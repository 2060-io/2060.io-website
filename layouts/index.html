Who we are

Independent. Technical. Long-Term.

2060 is a small, senior team distributed across the world. We are the people who, after many years at the Internet Identity Workshop, invented the concept of Verifiable Trust, and then wrote the specifications, seeded the reference implementations, and co-founded the Verana Foundation to steward the work as a public good.

We do not sell a platform. We write protocols. We ship open source. We operate the decentralized networks those protocols depend on. Our revenue comes from research, consulting, and managed operation, never from gatekeeping the technologies we invent.

When you work with 2060, you work directly with spec authors, maintainers, and operators. Not a sales team.

What we believe

Principles We Don't Compromise

Six principles guide every engagement, every spec, and every line of code.

Decentralization
No single entity should decide who is allowed to exist online. Trust is cryptographic, portable, and ecosystem-governed.
Privacy
Personal data is not a business model. Selective disclosure and local-first computation are baseline requirements.
Sovereignty
Individuals own their identity and data. Organizations own their services and infrastructure.
Verifiability
"Trust me" is not a protocol. Every claim is independently verifiable; every governance rule is public.
Open source, open standards
We publish specifications so the work outlives us and competitors become collaborators.
Ship the code
A specification without a working implementation is a suggestion. Every spec we author comes with a production reference implementation.

Standards

We Build on Open Standards

2060 does not reinvent protocols. We implement them, contribute back, and extend them where real deployments expose gaps. Every component we ship is built on open, interoperable standards maintained by global standards bodies, and we are active participants in those bodies.

Interoperable by design. No vendor lock-in. Ever.
